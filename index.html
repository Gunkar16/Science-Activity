Electrolysis uses an electric current to split water into hydrogen and oxygen. The process occurs in an electrolytic cell with two electrodes‚Äîan anode and a cathode. Oxygen forms at the anode through oxidation, while hydrogen forms at the cathode through reduction. The electric current helps ions move, allowing positively charged hydrogen ions (protons) to combine with electrons and generate hydrogen gas. Electrolysis provides a clean and efficient way to obtain hydrogen for energy applications.

Steam reforming is a commonly employed technique for large-scale hydrogen production. It entails heating hydrocarbon feedstocks, such as natural gas or methane, in the presence of steam and a catalyst. The high-temperature reaction breaks down the hydrocarbons into hydrogen gas, carbon monoxide, and traces of other gases, forming a versatile syngas mixture that can be purified to obtain high-purity hydrogen for various industrial applications and clean energy purposes.
